Is Your "Savings" Insurance a High-Fee Trap?

"Death Planning" vs. "Life Planning"

Many "savings-type" life insurance policies (貯蓄型保険) are sold as a disciplined way to save for the future while getting protection. But are you planning for your life, or only for after your death?

  • High Hidden Costs: A significant portion of your premium doesn't go toward your savings; it pays for the insurance cost, agent commissions, and company overhead. These fees act as a major drag on your growth.
  • Dismal Returns: The actual return on your money is often far lower than projected, sometimes even failing to keep pace with inflation. Your "savings" can lose purchasing power over time.
  • The "Better Off Dead" Problem: In many cases, these policies provide the best financial outcome only if the insured person passes away. For those who live, the surrender value (解約返戻金) can be shockingly low.

Scenario 2: The "Hidden Catch" Policy

The Reality: After all company costs and fees, almost 30% of that projected interest 'disappears'. The actual growth on the money is far, far less than what was advertised.
